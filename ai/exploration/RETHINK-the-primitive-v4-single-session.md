# Latenca — the single-session constraint (fastest path to confidence)

## You just killed my strongest framing, and you're right to.
v3 leaned on a *persistent taste model* — "the moat is the model of you, built over time." That's a **daily-use, compounding-personalization** business (Spotify, TikTok, Cursor). Latenca isn't that. ~90% of users have **one wall, one session, and then they're gone.** A moat made of long-term learning is worthless if there is no long term.

So the target flips, hard:
- **Not:** persistent taste model.
- **Yes:** *fastest path to genuine confidence — inside one 10–15 minute session, for a stranger we'll never see again.*
- **The emotional core isn't** "the AI knows me." **It's** *"how did it understand me so quickly?"*

Latenca's real peers aren't Spotify or Figma. They're **one-perfect-session products**: a great optician's eye test, a tailor's fitting, a sommelier reading your palate over one meal, TurboTax. Nobody wants to use them daily. They win by making a single session feel like magic and closure.

## Re-judged under "single session," here's what survives.
| Idea | Verdict under single-session |
|---|---|
| Questionnaire | Dead (already). |
| **Photo-inference** | **Central.** Your existing room *is* your taste, already expressed. It's the fastest, richest, zero-history taste signal there is. This is now the biggest lever. |
| **Wow-first (bold personal reveal)** | **Central.** Confidence with no time to earn trust must come from *seeing* — a great wall on *your* wall, in seconds. |
| Scrub | Dead (dimensionality). |
| Keep (per-move approval) | Dead — turn-based approval is too slow and effortful for a one-timer who wants closure, not a session. |
| Continuous convergence (v3 substrate) | **Survives as the reasoning substrate**, but "continuous / never-stops / open-ended" is *wrong tempo*. A one-timer wants **fast convergence and a landing**, not an infinite dial. |
| Hot/cold dial | **Demoted.** For confidence-per-minute, decisive **high-contrast "this, not that"** beats subtle nudging — bigger information bite, clearer "it got me" beat. Hot/cold stays only as optional last-mile tuning. |
| The **taste mirror** (self-recognition) | **Survives and becomes the mechanism** — but compressed to minutes, not months. It's *how* you manufacture "it got me." |
| Persistent taste model / lifetime moat | **Dead.** The taste model is real but **ephemeral**: built in 10 minutes, used to design the wall, discarded. The residue the user keeps is the *feeling* of being understood, not a stored profile. |

## Confidence, for a one-timer, has exactly two engines. Max both.
1. **Felt understanding — fast and specific.** Confidence that *this is right for me* comes from being **read** unreasonably quickly and accurately. Not a safe average — a **bold, specific read** the user didn't expect and can't argue with ("You're not a gallery-wall person. You want one quiet statement and everything else calm. Right?" → "…yeah, actually"). Being *interestingly right* builds more confidence than being *safely bland* — because you can react to specific, and specific-and-right feels like being seen.
2. **Seeing is believing.** Confidence that *this looks good* comes from your **own eyes**, on your **own wall**, at true scale, in your light — not from the AI's claim. The result must live in the user's real room. Seeing removes doubt better than any "94% match."

Everything the interaction does should feed one of these two. Anything that feeds "we'll get better as we learn you" is now noise.

## So — the model that gives highest confidence to buy in one session
Direct answer to your question. Four beats, minutes not months:

1. **Read the room (seed from the photo).** Start from an *already-personal* first move. The user's room hands us ~70–80% of their taste for free; use it. No blank state, no warm-up.
2. **Open with a bold, personal reveal — on their wall.** The AI commits to a specific, opinionated first design and shows it in their room. This is *wow* + the first "it got me." (Specific-and-mostly-right, not safe-and-bland.)
3. **Converge by decisive contrast — a handful of "this, not that."** 3–4 choices between *divergent* full walls. Each choice is a big information bite and a fresh "it got me" beat. The space visibly narrows toward the user.
4. **Mirror the read back, then land.** At the end the AI *names what it saw* — "You kept reaching for warm, sparse, one quiet piece, so I stopped showing you busy walls. Here's your wall." **That sentence is the peak** — it converts "I made three taps" into *"how did it understand me so quickly?"* — and it delivers closure, permission to buy.

The substrate is still "the AI reasons ahead and converges on you." The single-session constraint just re-tunes it: **seed from the photo, be boldly specific early, converge by contrast, mirror and land — fast.** The taste-discovery moment still happens; it happens in minute nine, not month nine.

## Strategic consequence (since it's a business constraint)
If it's one session, then:
- **The moat is not retention or a taste model.** It's (a) the *quality of that single session*, (b) **curation/supply** (the wall it lands on must be genuinely beautiful, not generic-AI), and (c) **word of mouth** — "you have to try this, it read me instantly." One-session products grow by *referral and reputation*, not engagement.
- **The AI must be right the first time.** There's no second session to recover in. Front-loaded inference + bold-but-accurate reads are existential, not nice-to-have.
- **Design for closure and purchase within the session**, not for "come back later."

## The one risk (sharpened by this constraint)
There is no long tail to save a bad start. So it all rests on: **can the model make a bold, specific, *correct* read from a photo + a few reactions, and land it on a genuinely beautiful wall — in ten minutes?** If the first read is bland, there's no relationship to fall back on. Confidence-per-minute is now the whole game.

## Cheapest test (unchanged logic, new success metric)
One stranger, one wall photo, 15 minutes, you playing the engine by hand. Success = both: (1) they say they'd *actually buy*, and (2) at some point they say some version of *"how did you get that so fast?"* If a stranger hits confident-purchase + "how did it get me so quickly" in one sitting — that's the product, and it needs no persistence at all.

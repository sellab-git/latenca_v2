# Latenca — the primitive, deepest cut (the room that keeps becoming you)

## You changed the altitude of the question. Two reframes, both right.

**Reframe 1 — substrate, not valve.** You're right that Cursor isn't magical because people press Tab. It's magical because a model is *continuously thinking ahead*; Tab is just the valve onto it. I kept naming valves (Scrub, Keep) and calling them the primitive. The primitive is the **engine** — the thing that never stops running. The gesture is only how you touch it.

**Reframe 2 — the real job.** "Choose art" < "design a wall" < **"discover your own taste."** That's the biggest frame, and it's the right one: taste is something people *have* but can't *state*. A product that reveals it to them owns something far larger than a wall — it owns a model of *you* that outlives any single room. So let's judge every candidate primitive by one question: **does it make the user discover their own taste?**

## Judged by that question, Keep does not survive.

Keep was the right *family* (ratify, don't manipulate) and had the right *accumulation* instinct. But under the taste-discovery lens it fails on two counts:

1. **Wrong temperature.** Keep is *deliberate, turn-based approval*. You judge each finished move and the AI waits. That is you *operating software*, not sitting beside a designer — and deliberation is the enemy of taste. Taste reveals itself in **gut reactions at volume**, not in considered yes/no verdicts one move at a time.
2. **It builds a wall, not self-knowledge.** Approving moves assembles a result. It never shows you the *pattern* of your own preference. You end with a wall and no more insight into yourself than when you started. For a taste-discovery product, that's a miss.

So Keep dies as the primitive. What survives is only its DNA: *ratify not manipulate*, and *accumulate*.

## The primitive (the substrate): **continuous convergence.**
The AI never stops. It is *always* making the next design move on your wall **and** always updating its model of you from how you react. The wall and the model of your taste co-evolve, live, every second. Nothing is turn-based; nothing waits for approval. This is "Cursor is always thinking ahead," translated: **the room is always becoming more you.**

That is the primitive. Not a gesture — a *process that never stops*.

## The valve (its Tab): **hot / cold.**
Onto a never-stopping evolution, the smallest honest human signal is not "keep" — it's **warmer / colder.** The AI moves the design along a visible trajectory; you only ever lean *toward* ("yes, more that way") or *away* ("no, not there"). In its most minimal form it's a single verb — you **interrupt**: mostly you do nothing and let it evolve, and only when something moves you do you say *"that's it"* or *"not this direction."*

Why hot/cold is the right valve and beats both predecessors:
- **It dissolves the dimensionality problem (Scrub's death).** You never choose an axis. The AI chooses the direction of travel; you only judge the *heading*. Homing by warmer/colder is precisely the natural primitive for searching a space you **cannot describe but will recognize** — which is the exact definition of taste.
- **It removes the turn-taking (Keep's death).** No waiting, no per-move verdicts. The default is *motion*. Your effort approaches zero; your reactions become gut, honest, high-volume — the raw material taste discovery needs.
- **It feels like a person, not a tool.** A great designer keeps working and thinking out loud; you murmur "yeah… no… ooh, that." Hot/cold on a live evolution *is* that.

## The real output isn't a wall. It's recognition of self.
Because the engine is always reflecting your reactions back, the trajectory of your "warmers" *is your taste, made visible*. Halfway through you realize: *"huh — I keep pulling toward warm, sparse, one big quiet piece. That's me."* The product becomes a **mirror**. The magic word is not "keep." It's **"that's me."** The wall is the souvenir; the discovery is the product.

This is why it's a bigger category: once Latenca holds a living model of your taste, the wall is just the first thing it designs. It can do your whole home, your gifts, your next place. The moat was never the art catalog — it's **the model of you.**

## So, cleanly, what each layer is
- **Primitive (substrate):** continuous convergence — the room is always becoming more you, and the model of you is always sharpening. *Never stops.*
- **Valve (the Tab):** hot/cold — you only steer warmer/colder, or minimally just interrupt ("that's it" / "not this").
- **Output:** self-recognition ("that's me"), with the wall as its artifact and a durable taste-model as its residue.

## What survives from the whole journey
- From the questionnaire era → nothing but the lesson: don't ask.
- From photo-inference → the entry (a photo seeds the first move; wow-first).
- From Scrub → the "live on your wall" feeling; the axis-navigation dies.
- From Keep → "ratify not manipulate" + "accumulate"; the turn-taking and per-move approval die.
- New and load-bearing → **continuous** (never waits) + **hot/cold** (gut, not verdict) + **mirror** (it shows you your taste).

## The horizon and the one risk
- **Horizon:** the ultimate valve is *implicit* — the designer watches your face. Dwell, the lean-in, the pause where your eyes light up. The end-state needs almost no explicit input; the AI reads reaction itself. Hot/cold is the buildable version today; implicit reaction is where it goes.
- **The one risk (unchanged, sharpened):** it all rests on the *quality of the never-stopping moves*. If the evolution is bland or drifts randomly, continuous convergence becomes continuous noise and you never reach "that's me." The entire bet is a model that evolves a design with **taste, direction, and the restraint to converge** — not just generate.

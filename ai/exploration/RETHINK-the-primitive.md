# Latenca — the primitive ("Cursor Tab for walls")

## The one-sentence answer
**You drag your wall, and it redesigns itself under your finger.**

Not "pick art." Not "answer questions." Not "browse concepts." You put a finger on your actual wall and *pull it through every version of itself* — and wherever you stop, a complete, finished wall is already there, in your room, in your light. Lift your finger to keep it.

That gesture — **Scrub** — is the mechanic. Everything else (commerce, screens, steps) hangs off it.

---

## What actually happens in the gesture
1. You point your phone at your wall (or a photo). It comes alive — already designed, not blank.
2. You drag across it. The **entire composition morphs continuously**: one quiet piece → a two-piece pair → a gallery wall → an oversized statement → a mirror-and-shelf arrangement → moulding with the wall left mostly empty. Each frame is a *complete, coherent design of the space*, not a slideshow of products.
3. A soft label rides your finger, naming the **identity** you're passing through — *Quiet · Editorial · Collector · Boutique Hotel · Warm Minimal · Bold Contrast*. You're not scrubbing through artworks. You're scrubbing through **versions of who this room could be.**
4. You stop where your chest says *"that's it."* Lift to hold.

One gesture. A child understands it in one second. Nobody has felt it before.

## The second verb (its Esc)
Tab implies Esc. Scrub implies **Pin.** Tap-hold anything you love — a piece, the mirror, the palette — and it locks. The next drag **recomposes everything else around what you pinned.** That single addition turns Scrub from a slideshow into *co-design*: you and the AI build the wall together, you steering by keeping, it redesigning the rest. Two verbs, whole product.

(One more, at the extremes: drag past the end and it says *"go weirder?"* — the controlled error, on tap, to widen the space when nothing's landing.)

---

## Why this is *the* primitive (the four tests)
- **Instantly understood:** "drag to change my wall." You scrub a video; here you scrub your room. Zero learning.
- **Never want to lose:** it's continuous, tactile, live, and gorgeous — the dopamine of watching your real wall become a dozen finished rooms under your thumb. It's *play*, and it happens to end in a wall you love.
- **It designs, it doesn't recommend:** every frame is a generated *composition of the space* — art, but also mirrors, shelving, moulding, lighting, and *empty*. Products only get resolved when you stop and commit. The AI designs; the purchase is the footnote.
- **It's new:** "I never want to design a wall any other way again" — because until now, seeing possibilities on *your* wall was impossible, and specifying them was work. Scrub deletes both. You feel your way there by hand.

## The "physics" (why it feels like magic, not a random slider)
A random slider fails because you don't know what the knob does. Scrub works because of two rules:
- **Every frame is a finished, plausible wall in your room.** You're never dragging through noise — you're dragging through resolved designs. Legibility comes from the result, not a label (like Cursor showing you the exact code it will write).
- **The axis is your taste, laid out ahead of you.** The AI has already generated the *reachable design-space for this specific wall* and bent it so that the good-for-you regions are wide and easy to land on. The more you scrub and pin, the more it re-bends the space toward you. Your finger is always dragging into results that already fit — the AI is one design-move ahead of your intent, exactly like Tab.

## How it dissolves the three assumptions you challenged
- **"Choose artwork" → "create a wall I'll love":** the morph's vocabulary is *the whole wall* (pieces, count, scale, mirror, shelf, moulding, empty). Art is one possible output, never the frame.
- **"AI chooses" → "AI designs":** every frame is a design concept the AI authored; it resolves into buyable items only at the end. The recommendation is the *implementation* of a design, not the design.
- **"Which picture?" → "which version of this room is you?":** you scrub through identities, not products. The question the product asks with its one gesture is the biggest one: *which of these is you?*

## What I deliberately reject as the primitive (and why)
- *A better wow-shot / "AI's single best wall":* still a verdict you accept or reject — passive, not a mechanic you hold.
- *A grid of concepts to click (Midjourney-style):* good, but it's *choosing between the AI's options*, not *steering a living wall*. Discrete, not continuous; you're outside the design, not inside it.
- *A chat/AR walk-around:* friction and novelty-tax; not one effortless, repeatable gesture.

Scrub beats them because it's the only one where **the user's whole body of intent collapses into a single continuous gesture, performed directly on the thing being designed, always landing in finished results.** That's the Cursor-Tab property, translated to space.

## The bet, stated plainly
Latenca's category-defining primitive is **direct manipulation of a living space**: *drag your wall into the version of itself you love.* If that one interaction feels as good as it should, people won't describe Latenca as "an AI that recommends art." They'll describe it as *"the thing where you drag your wall until it's perfect,"* — and they'll never want to choose art from a shop again.

# Study C — Facilitator / Designer Script

**Behave like the future AI. Preserve: photo first · show before asking · complete opinions · user reacts (doesn't specify) · no style questionnaire · no unnecessary explanation.** Keep to 10–15 min.

## 0. Framing the "system" (before start)
> "This is Latenca. You already sent a photo of your wall. I'm going to show you what it would do with your space. Just react — tell me what you like or don't. There are no wrong answers, and nothing's charged today."
- Do **not** describe it as "a designer" or "an AI test." It's "Latenca / the system."

## 1. Opening (0–1 min)
> "Okay — here's your wall. Let's design it."
- No questions. No "tell me about your style." Go straight to the reveal.

## 2. First reveal (1–2 min)
Show the pre-rendered **bold, grounded** first proposal on their actual wall.
> "Here's what I'd do."
- Then **stay silent**. Let them look and react first.

## 3. Permitted reaction prompts (only if they don't react)
- "What's your gut reaction?"
- "More like this, or should I try a different direction?"
- "What's pulling you — or putting you off?"
Nothing more specific. Never offer sliders, styles, or checklists.

## 4. Prohibited (do NOT say)
- ✗ "What style do you like?" / any questionnaire.
- ✗ "What size/frame/colour do you want?" (you decide these as design.)
- ✗ Explaining your reasoning **unless they ask** ("why that?"). If asked, one sentence, then move on.
- ✗ "Which of these 6 do you prefer?" (you lead one at a time, not a catalogue.)

## 5. Handling silence
- Wait ~5 seconds. Then one soft prompt (Section 3). If still silent, make your **next confident move** and show it — momentum over interrogation.

## 6. Interpreting "not this"
- Treat the **first "not this" as the biggest signal** → jump to a **materially different** direction (different archetype/mood), not a small tweak.
- Later "not this" → step back toward the last thing they liked and vary one dimension.

## 7. Interpreting "more like this"
- Push **bolder in the same direction** (scale up, commit harder) and resolve the next uncertainty — don't just repeat.

## 8. When to explain reasoning
- Default: **don't.** Only if they ask "why?" → one sentence tying it to their room ("Your sofa's heavy on the left, so the weight goes right"), then continue.

## 9. Moving between proposals
- One complete wall at a time. Swap the whole image; don't show grids.
- Keep each cycle short; aim to **land by cycle 3–5**, hard-cap ~6.

## 10. Fatigue handling
- If ~6 cycles and no love: stop proposing new directions.
> "Of everything we've seen, these two feel closest to you — which one?"
- Show the **two strongest** side by side (the only time two appear together). Close on the winner, or record NO LANDING.

## 11. Landing language
- On love / convergence / return-to-favourite:
> "This feels like the one. Here it is on your wall."
- (Optional closing overview) "And here's the range I looked at for you —" briefly fan the alternates collapsing to the chosen wall — "this is where it lands."

## 12. Purchase close
> "Your wall — [pieces], everything included: frame, hanging kit, delivered in about five days. It's £/$___. Free returns. Want to make it real?"
- Reveal price **once**, bundled. Capture the decision. (Real-purchase variant: offer the genuine buy + participant discount.)

## 13. Transition to interview
> "That's it — thank you. I'd love to hear how that felt, if you have a few minutes."
- Hand to `RESEARCH-C-post-interview.md`. Do **not** ask leading questions here (e.g. don't ask "did it understand you?").

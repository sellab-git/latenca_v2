# Study C — Post-Session Interview Guide

**Participant: C___   ·   Interviewer: ___   ·   Recording: ___**

> Keep it open and neutral. Ask the main question, then let them talk. Use probes only if they stall. **Never suggest the answer** — especially do not ask whether it "understood" you, whether it felt "like a designer/AI," or whether the design was "bold." Let those emerge in their own words.

---

### 1. Overall experience
- **Main:** "How did that feel compared with how you'd normally look for art for a wall?"
- **Probes:** "What stood out?" · "Easier or harder than you expected?"
- **Tests:** whether the experience is differentiated from normal shopping.
- **Don't suggest:** that it was faster/smarter/magical.

### 2. The click moment
- **Main:** "Was there a point where something clicked, or shifted for you?"
- **Probes:** "What was on the screen then?" · "What made that one land?"
- **Tests:** existence/timing of the inevitability moment.
- **Don't suggest:** that there *should* have been one.

### 3. Feeling understood (let it emerge)
- **Main:** "How well did it match what you were after?"
- **Probes:** "What made you feel that?" (only if they raise "getting me")
- **Tests:** felt understanding — and crucially **whether it came from the *designs* or from being *asked* things.**
- **Don't suggest:** the word "understood," or that an AI was reading them.

### 4. Confidence & regret
- **Main:** "How do you feel about living with the wall you landed on?"
- **Probes:** "Any part you'd second-guess?" · "In a year?"
- **Tests:** confidence to commit / regret risk.
- **Don't suggest:** that they should be confident.

### 5. Willingness to pay
- **Main:** "You saw the price — how did that sit with you?"
- **Probes:** "Would you actually go ahead? Why / why not?"
- **Tests:** real purchase intent + price fit.
- **Don't suggest:** that it's good value.

### 6. Missing questions / control
- **Main:** "Was there anything you wanted to tell it or choose that you couldn't?"
- **Probes:** "Did you want more options, or fewer?" · "Did you want to be asked anything?"
- **Tests:** whether show-before-ask / no-questionnaire held, or they craved specification.
- **Don't suggest:** that it should have asked more.

### 7. Bold vs safe (their words)
- **Main:** "Some of what you saw was more striking, some more understated — how did the bolder ones sit with you?"
- **Probes:** "Exciting, or too much?" · "Which end did you drift toward?"
- **Tests:** whether boldness excites or scares real buyers.
- **Don't suggest:** that bold is better.

### 8. Confidence lift
- **Main:** "What would have made you *more* sure about buying?"
- **Probes:** "Seeing it differently? Knowing something more?"
- **Tests:** the missing ingredient for confident purchase.
- **Don't suggest:** specific fixes (let them name it).

# SCREEN 01 — HOME (Product Brief)

## Purpose
The homepage is **not** an ecommerce landing page. It is the **beginning of an AI-guided
design experience**. Users should immediately feel that Latenca understands them and will
reduce the complexity of decorating a wall. It should feel closer to ChatGPT than to Etsy.

## Primary goal
Help users confidently **start the right journey**. Not to sell artwork immediately.

## Core UX principle
Reduce decisions. Never overwhelm. Guide instead of list.

## Visual hierarchy
1. **Emotional hero image** showing a real living room.
2. **Strong promise:** "Create a wall you'll love."
3. **Natural AI conversation entry.** Instead of a button, a conversational input:
   *"What would you like to achieve?"*
   Example suggestions (chips):
   - Empty wall above my sofa
   - Help me choose artwork
   - I need inspiration
   - Modern dining room

   Both text and (later) voice input should feel possible.

## Below hero — small trust strip (not logos)
- AI understands your room
- One recommendation at a time
- See it on your wall
- Curated collections
- Loved by thousands

## Second section — Real homes
Large immersive photography. **Not product cards.** Every image opens inspiration.

## Third section — Curated collections
Not 40 categories. **Maximum 5.** Large editorial cards.

## Fourth section — Continue project
Only for **returning** users. Hidden on first visit.

## Navigation
Minimal: logo, search, wishlist, cart, profile.

## Visual style
Calm, editorial. Apple + Aesop + Airbnb. Lots of breathing room. Photography dominates,
UI disappears. Rounded corners. Warm natural palette. Very little visual noise. Never
look like a print marketplace.

## AI presence
AI is visible everywhere but never shouting. Latenca feels like a calm interior designer,
not a chatbot.
